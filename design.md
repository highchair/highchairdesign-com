### Revenue Solutions Inc. (RSI)

**Tags:** Oomph, Government, Design Systems, UX/UI consulting

## 2020
{:.h6.underlined}

### State of Rhode Island: Quahog Design System
{:.h4}

[State of RI case study at Oomph](https://www.oomphinc.com/work/state-government-platform-inclusive-design-system/)<span class="external">⤴</span>

**Role at Oomph: Director of UX & Design, strategist, front-end developer, digital accessibility-specialist.** During COVID, I worked with the team to develop a new state-wide <span class="abbr">CMS</span> distribution and design system with five color themes, each with light and dark modes, and over 30 visual components, all with a high accessibility conformance. In four months, the State rolled out the first handful of sites to communicate important health information. After five years, over 100 agencies use the system. In 2025, I am leading an audit and design refresh.

**Tags:** Oomph, Government, Design System, UX/UI consulting


## 2014
{:.h6.underlined}

### [Python.org](https://www.python.org/)<span class="external">⤴</span>
{:.h4}

**Role at Project Evolution: Creative Director, UX architect, front-end developer.** Hard to believe, but more than 10 years later, the interface of Python looks the same as it did when we launched its brand refresh. It looked dated now, but the leap from where it came from was massive and still remains an upgrade I am proud of.

**Tags:** Project Evolution, Designer/Developer, UX/UI consulting


### [Jeff Carpenter](https://jcarpenterstudio.com/)<span class="external">⤴</span>

**Independent Role: Strategist, designer, & custom CMS developer.** Jeff and I worked together on a few projects starting around 2007 before we redesigned his art portfolio website. Previous projects included Reconnect.US, an art exhibition; Arts Express, a Fall River, Massachusetts-based non-profit; and Fall River Storefront Artists, a storefront revitalization project. Jeff and I continue to work together on his digital presence.

**Tags:** Freelance, Designer/Developer, Artist Portfolio


## 2003
{:.h6.underlined}

### [Preserve RI](https://www.preserveri.org/)<span class="external">⤴</span>
{:.h4}

**Independent Role: Digital and Print Designer, Brand Strategist.** Our work with Preserve RI brought back a previous logo iteration and modernized it. The five acorns — one for each of Rhode Island’s five counties — had been shelved but proved to be a lovely, unique, and beloved part of their brand identity. In combination with bright greens and a modern semi-serif font, the logo lockup more than 20 years later still reflects some of our original thinking.

**Tags:** Freelance, Print design, Brand design
